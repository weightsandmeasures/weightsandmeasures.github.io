Kenya Weights Measures

Kenya Weights and Measures Regulations

Regulatory Framework Overview

Kenya maintains a comprehensive legal metrology system to ensure fair trade practices and consumer protection in commercial transactions involving measurement.

Governing Legislation

Weights and Measures Act (Cap 513)

The Weights and Measures Act is the primary legislation governing weighing and measuring instruments used in trade in Kenya. This Act establishes:

  • Legal requirements for measuring equipment used in trade
  • Standards for accuracy and verification
  • Penalties for non-compliance and fraud
  • Authority for weights and measures officials

Key Provisions

  1. Equipment Standards

    • All weighing and measuring equipment used for trade must meet prescribed standards
    • Instruments must be type-approved before use
    • Regular verification required
    • Equipment must bear valid verification stamps

  2. Verification Requirements

    • Initial verification before first use
    • Periodic re-verification at prescribed intervals
    • Verification conducted by authorized officials
    • Equipment sealed to prevent tampering

  3. Trading Standards

    • Sellers must provide accurate measurements
    • Weighing must be done in customer's presence when requested
    • Scales must be clearly visible to customer
    • Prohibited to use un-verified or tampered equipment

  4. Enforcement

    • Weights and Measures Inspectors authorized to conduct inspections
    • Power to test equipment at business premises
    • Authority to seize non-compliant equipment
    • Penalties for violations

Regulatory Bodies

1. Weights & Measures Directorate

Parent Organization: State Department For Trade, Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry

Primary Responsibilities:

  • Ensures fair trade practices and consumer protection
  • Maintains uniform and accurate legal metrology systems
  • Conducts inspections of weighing and measuring instruments
  • Ensures compliance with Weights and Measures Act Cap 513

Key Functions:

  • Traceability: Provides traceability to County Legal Metrology systems through calibration of standards
  • Equipment Testing: Testing and verification of measuring equipment
  • Licensing: Issues licenses for:
    • Weighing and measuring equipment technicians
    • Equipment manufacturers
    • Equipment sellers
    • Repairers of weighing equipment
  • Registration: Maintains register of manufacturers and sellers
  • Enforcement: Conducts compliance inspections and enforcement actions

2. Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS)

Role: Government regulatory body under the Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry

Mandates:

  • Prepare standards relating to products, measurements, materials, and processes
  • Promote standardization in commerce and industry
  • Product certification and quality assurance
  • Testing and calibration services

Relevance to Weighing Equipment:

  • Sets technical standards for weighing instruments
  • Provides type approval for equipment models
  • Operates testing laboratories
  • Issues product certification marks
  • Maintains national measurement standards

Equipment Requirements for Trade Use

Mandatory Requirements

Every piece of weighing equipment used for trade must:

  1. Bear a Valid Stamp of Verification

    • Stamped by authorized weights and measures official
    • Shows year of verification
    • Indicates it meets accuracy requirements
    • Must be clearly visible

  2. Have Type Approval

    • Equipment model must be approved for use in Kenya
    • Type approval obtained from KEBS or Weights & Measures authority
    • Demonstrates design meets legal requirements
    • Valid for specific equipment model

  3. Maintain Accuracy Standards

    • Must meet specified tolerance limits
    • Tested at time of verification
    • Regular re-verification ensures continued accuracy
    • Must remain in approved condition

  4. Display Required Markings

    • Manufacturer identification
    • Serial number
    • Capacity (maximum weight)
    • Division (increment)
    • Accuracy class designation

  5. Protection Against Tampering

    • Security seals on adjustment mechanisms
    • Breaking seal voids verification
    • Unauthorized modifications prohibited
    • Seals applied by authorized officials

Prohibited Equipment

The following are prohibited for use in trade:

  • Un-verified equipment (lacking valid stamps)
  • Equipment with broken verification seals
  • Tampered or modified equipment
  • Equipment exceeding tolerance limits
  • Un-approved equipment types
  • Damaged or defective instruments

Verification Process

Initial Verification

When Required:

  • Before first use for trade
  • After equipment repair or modification
  • After equipment has been sealed by the owner
  • When verification mark is missing or damaged

Process:

  1. Application: Submit request to Weights & Measures office
  2. Documentation: Provide equipment details, purchase records
  3. On-Site Testing: Official tests equipment using certified standards
  4. Accuracy Check: Verify equipment meets tolerance requirements
  5. Sealing: If approved, official applies verification stamp and seals
  6. Certificate: Issue of verification certificate
  7. Record: Equipment registered in verification register

Testing Points:

  • Zero point accuracy
  • Span accuracy (at maximum capacity)
  • Intermediate points (typically at 1/3, 1/2, 2/3 capacity)
  • Repeatability
  • Corner error (for platform scales)

Periodic Re-Verification

Frequency:

  • Generally required annually
  • May be more frequent for heavily used equipment
  • Specified by regulations for different equipment types

Purpose:

  • Ensure continued accuracy
  • Detect wear or damage
  • Confirm no tampering has occurred
  • Maintain consumer protection

Triggers for Inspection:

  • Scheduled periodic verification
  • Customer complaints
  • Routine inspections by officials
  • Random spot checks
  • Investigation of suspected fraud

Licensing and Registration

Equipment Technician Licensing

Who Needs a License:

  • Persons repairing weighing equipment
  • Technicians servicing measuring instruments
  • Businesses providing calibration services

Requirements (Typical):

  • Technical training in metrology
  • Practical experience
  • Examination or competency assessment
  • Registration fee
  • Annual renewal

Responsibilities:

  • Maintain equipment in approved condition
  • Use certified calibration weights
  • Keep proper service records
  • Report equipment issues to authorities
  • Comply with technical standards

Manufacturer and Seller Registration

Registration Requirements:

  • Business registration documents
  • Details of equipment types sold/manufactured
  • Evidence of sourcing (for sellers)
  • Manufacturing facility inspection (for manufacturers)
  • Registration fee

Obligations:

  • Sell only type-approved equipment
  • Provide verification information to buyers
  • Maintain sales records
  • Cooperate with inspections
  • Report defective equipment

Compliance and Enforcement

Inspection Authority

Weights and Measures Inspectors have authority to:

  • Enter business premises during business hours
  • Inspect and test weighing equipment
  • Examine records related to measurements
  • Take samples for testing
  • Seize non-compliant equipment
  • Issue compliance notices
  • Institute prosecutions

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Violations of the Weights and Measures Act may result in:

Administrative Penalties:

  • Notices to repair or replace equipment
  • Suspension of verification
  • Equipment confiscation
  • Business closure orders

Criminal Penalties:

  • Fines (amounts specified in Act and regulations)
  • Imprisonment for serious offenses
  • Both fine and imprisonment
  • Additional penalties for repeat offenders

Common Violations:

  • Using un-verified equipment
  • Tampering with equipment or verification marks
  • Fraudulent weighing practices
  • Obstructing inspectors
  • Failing to comply with notices

Consumer Rights

Consumers have the right to:

  • Request verification of weight measurement
  • See the scale during weighing
  • Lodge complaints about suspected fraud
  • Request inspection of trader's equipment
  • Receive accurate measurements

Industry-Specific Requirements

Retail Trade

Requirements:

  • Scales must be visible to customer
  • Price computing scales must show weight, unit price, and total
  • Zero reading before placing goods
  • Customer can request re-weighing

Agricultural Produce

Special Considerations:

  • Grain moisture content may affect pricing
  • Weighing of bulk agricultural products
  • Mobile weighing services for farms
  • Seasonal verification demands

Scrap Metal and Recyclables

Regulatory Focus:

  • Higher scrutiny due to fraud history
  • Frequent inspections
  • Strict enforcement
  • Licensing requirements

Transport and Logistics

Weighbridge Requirements:

  • Higher capacity instruments
  • More stringent accuracy requirements
  • Detailed transaction records
  • Often integrated with regulatory systems

International Alignment

OIML Recommendations

Kenya's standards align with International Organization of Legal Metrology (OIML) recommendations, particularly:

  • OIML R 76: Non-automatic weighing instruments
  • OIML R 111: Weights of classes E1, E2, F1, F2, M1, M2, M3

This alignment facilitates:

  • International trade
  • Import of approved equipment
  • Mutual recognition with other countries
  • Adoption of best practices

East African Community (EAC) Harmonization

As an EAC member, Kenya participates in:

  • Harmonization of metrology standards
  • Mutual recognition agreements
  • Regional cooperation on enforcement
  • Shared training and capacity building

Obtaining Services

Where to Apply

County Weights and Measures Offices:

  • Equipment verification
  • Inspection services
  • License applications
  • Complaints

KEBS Offices:

  • Type approval applications
  • Product certification
  • Technical standards information
  • Testing and calibration services

Required Documentation

For Verification:

  • Equipment purchase invoice
  • Type approval certificate (if applicable)
  • Previous verification certificate (for re-verification)
  • Business registration documents
  • Application fee payment

For Licensing:

  • Application form
  • Qualification certificates
  • Experience documentation
  • National ID or passport
  • License fee

Best Practices for Compliance

For Equipment Owners

  1. Regular Maintenance

    • Schedule periodic verification before expiry
    • Keep equipment clean and functional
    • Protect from damage and weather
    • Use only qualified technicians

  2. Record Keeping

    • Maintain verification certificates
    • Keep service and repair records
    • Document calibration activities
    • Retain transaction records

  3. Staff Training

    • Train staff on proper operation
    • Educate on legal requirements
    • Ensure awareness of customer rights
    • Promote integrity in measurements

  4. Facility Standards

    • Provide stable installation location
    • Ensure adequate lighting
    • Allow customer visibility of scale
    • Maintain clean weighing area

For New Equipment Purchases

  1. Verify Type Approval

    • Confirm model is approved for Kenya
    • Check with KEBS or Weights & Measures

  2. Choose Reputable Supplier

    • Select registered sellers
    • Verify warranty and service support
    • Ensure proper documentation

  3. Plan for Verification

    • Budget for initial verification
    • Schedule verification promptly
    • Allow time for installation settling

  4. Consider Total Cost

    • Initial purchase price
    • Verification fees
    • Annual re-verification costs
    • Maintenance and calibration

Key Takeaways

  • The Weights and Measures Act (Cap 513) governs measuring equipment used in trade
  • All trade equipment must bear valid verification stamps
  • Weights & Measures Directorate and KEBS share regulatory responsibilities
  • Equipment must be type-approved, verified, and periodically re-verified
  • Licensing required for technicians, manufacturers, and sellers
  • Strict penalties apply for fraud and non-compliance
  • Kenya's standards align with international (OIML) recommendations
  • Consumers have legal protections for accurate measurements
  • Compliance requires ongoing maintenance, documentation, and verification

Note: This information is based on publicly available regulations and may not reflect the most recent updates. For current requirements, fees, and procedures, contact the relevant authorities directly.
