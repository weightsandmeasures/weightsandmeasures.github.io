Weighbridges and Truck Scales

Introduction to Weighbridges

A weighbridge (also called a truck scale) is a large platform scale designed to weigh vehicles and their contents. Weighbridges are essential infrastructure for industries involving bulk transport of goods, including:

  • Agriculture and grain handling
  • Mining and quarrying
  • Waste management and recycling
  • Manufacturing and distribution
  • Port and shipping operations

Types of Weighbridges by Installation

There are three main types of weighbridges based on installation method: pit-mounted, surface-mounted, and portable.

1. Pit-Type (Pit-Mounted) Weighbridges

Description

A pit-type weighbridge is installed within a specially constructed pit dug into the ground, with the weighing deck positioned at ground level.

Key Characteristics

Design Features:

  • Weighbridge platform embedded in a pit or trench
  • Weighing surface completely level with surrounding ground
  • All mechanisms, wiring, and load cells located underground
  • Vehicles drive directly onto the platform without ramps

Advantages:

  • Space efficient: Takes up less total area since no approach ramps needed
  • Easy vehicle access: Level entry allows all vehicle types to be weighed
  • Professional appearance: Clean, flush-with-ground installation
  • No obstruction: Full utilization of surrounding space

Disadvantages:

  • Higher installation cost: Requires significant civil engineering work
    • Excavation and earthwork
    • Drainage system installation
    • Structural reinforcement
    • Waterproofing
  • Maintenance challenges:
    • Pit can fill with water, requiring drainage
    • Parts can rust in damp conditions
    • Access for inspection and repair is limited
    • Requires vigilant maintenance to prevent corrosion
  • Drainage requirements: Proper drainage system essential to prevent water accumulation

Best Suited For:

  • Permanent installations
  • Sites with space constraints
  • Locations with stable ground conditions
  • High-traffic commercial operations

Construction Requirements

  1. Excavation: Pit depth typically 1-1.5 meters
  2. Foundation: Reinforced concrete base and walls
  3. Drainage system: Pumps and drainage channels
  4. Waterproofing: To protect electronics and load cells
  5. Access provisions: Maintenance access points

2. Surface-Mounted (Pitless) Weighbridges

Description

A surface-mounted weighbridge sits on top of a solid foundation or concrete slab without requiring excavation, installed directly on the ground's surface.

Key Characteristics

Design Features:

  • Platform placed on existing ground surface
  • Requires approach ramps on both ends
  • All components above ground level
  • Framework visible and accessible

Advantages:

  • Lower installation cost: No excavation required
    • Simpler construction process
    • Faster installation (days vs. weeks)
    • Reduced civil engineering work
  • Easier maintenance:
    • All components easily accessible
    • Better ventilation reduces corrosion
    • Simpler inspection and repair
    • No drainage issues
  • Flexible placement:
    • Can be installed on various surfaces
    • Easier to relocate if needed
    • Suitable for temporary installations
  • Better drainage: Natural water run-off, no pit to flood

Disadvantages:

  • Larger footprint: Requires space for ramps
  • Height requirements: Vehicles must navigate inclined ramps
  • Site preparation: May need leveling and compaction
  • Visual impact: More visible structure

Best Suited For:

  • Temporary or semi-permanent installations
  • Sites with ample space
  • Locations requiring easy maintenance access
  • Areas with poor drainage or high water tables
  • Installations that may need to be relocated

Construction Requirements

  1. Concrete slab: Level, reinforced foundation
  2. Approach ramps: Typically 1:10 to 1:15 slope
  3. Drainage: Simple surface drainage around perimeter
  4. Anchoring: Securing platform to prevent movement

3. Portable Weighbridges

Description

Portable weighbridges are designed to be easily moved across different locations for temporary weighing applications.

Key Characteristics

Design Features:

  • Modular construction for easy assembly/disassembly
  • Lower framework adaptable to various surfaces
  • Can be placed on non-typical surfaces (dirt, gravel)
  • Lightweight components for transport

Advantages:

  • Mobility: Can be relocated as needed
  • Quick installation: Set up in hours rather than days
  • Versatile surfaces: Works on dirt, asphalt, concrete
  • Cost-effective: For temporary needs
  • No permanent construction: No civil works required

Accuracy:

  • Modern portable scales achieve ±1% accuracy
  • Comparable to permanent installations
  • Suitable for most commercial applications

Applications:

  • Construction sites (changing locations)
  • Temporary events
  • Seasonal operations (agriculture)
  • Remote locations
  • Emergency/backup weighing

Limitations:

  • Generally lower capacity than permanent installations
  • May require frequent recalibration
  • Less weather-resistant
  • More susceptible to ground settling

Types of Weighbridges by Operation

Static Weighbridges

Operation:

  • Vehicles stop completely on the platform
  • Weight measured when stationary
  • Most common type

Characteristics:

  • High accuracy: ±0.1% to ±0.2% of capacity
  • Simple operation: Driver stops, weight is recorded
  • Versatile: Suitable for all vehicle types
  • Time requirement: 30-60 seconds per vehicle

Applications:

  • Commercial transactions
  • Legal-for-trade weighing
  • Quality control
  • Inventory management

Dynamic (Weigh-in-Motion) Systems

Operation:

  • Vehicles weighed while moving at slow speed (5-15 km/h)
  • Continuous measurement as vehicle crosses

Characteristics:

  • Higher throughput: No need to stop
  • Lower accuracy: ±1% to ±2% typically
  • Requires special sensors: Dynamic load cells
  • More complex electronics: Real-time signal processing

Applications:

  • Pre-screening (before static weighing)
  • Traffic monitoring
  • Overload detection
  • High-volume operations where exact weight less critical

Axle Weighbridges

Operation:

  • Individual axles or axle groups weighed
  • Total weight calculated by summing axle weights

Characteristics:

  • Compact: Smaller platform
  • Portable: Easier to move
  • Lower cost: Than full-length weighbridges
  • Sequential process: Each axle weighed separately

Applications:

  • Enforcement (overload checking)
  • Portable operations
  • Space-constrained locations
  • Load distribution verification

Weighbridge Components

Platform Structure

Materials:

  • Steel deck: Most common, lighter weight
  • Concrete deck: Heavier, more durable, longer lasting
  • Composite: Combines benefits of both

Size Considerations:

  • Length: Typically 18-24 meters to accommodate trucks
  • Width: 3-3.5 meters for single lane
  • Capacity: 30 to 150 tonnes typical range

Load Cells

Configuration:

  • Number of cells: Typically 4, 6, or 8 load cells
  • Cell type: Compression or shear beam load cells
  • Capacity per cell: Chosen based on total capacity and truck configuration

Placement:

  • Strategically positioned for even load distribution
  • Protected from environmental exposure
  • Accessible for maintenance and calibration

Junction Boxes

Function:

  • Combine signals from multiple load cells
  • Allow trimming/corner adjustment
  • Protect connections from moisture

Features:

  • Weatherproof enclosure
  • Adjustable resistors for corner balancing
  • Surge protection
  • Terminal strips for connections

Indicator/Weighing Terminal

Functions:

  • Display weight reading
  • Store vehicle/transaction data
  • Interface with computer systems
  • Print weight tickets

Features:

  • Large LED or LCD display
  • Keyboard for data entry
  • Memory for transactions
  • Communication ports (RS-232, Ethernet, USB)
  • Software for reporting and analysis

Support Structure

Components:

  • Foundation: Concrete base supporting load cells
  • Ramps: For surface-mounted installations
  • Guardrails: Safety barriers
  • Traffic control: Lights, signs, barriers

Installation Considerations

Site Selection

Ground Conditions:

  • Stable soil with adequate bearing capacity
  • Low water table (especially for pit-type)
  • Level terrain or easily leveled
  • Good drainage characteristics

Operational Factors:

  • Adequate approach and departure space
  • Traffic flow patterns
  • Proximity to operations (minimizes truck travel)
  • Security considerations

Utilities:

  • Electrical power availability
  • Communication lines (if needed)
  • Lighting for 24-hour operations

Environmental Protection

Weatherproofing:

  • Load cell sealing against moisture
  • Junction box enclosures
  • Cable conduits and glands
  • Drainage systems

Lightning Protection:

  • Grounding system
  • Surge protection devices
  • Lightning arrestors

Corrosion Prevention:

  • Protective coatings on structural steel
  • Stainless steel components in corrosive environments
  • Regular maintenance and inspection

Capacity and Accuracy

Capacity Selection

Factors to Consider:

  • Maximum vehicle weight to be weighed
  • Legal weight limits for roads
  • Safety margin (typically 25-50% above expected maximum)
  • Future expansion needs

Common Capacities:

  • 30 tonnes: Light commercial vehicles
  • 60 tonnes: Standard trucks
  • 80-100 tonnes: Heavy-duty applications
  • 150+ tonnes: Mining, port operations

Accuracy Requirements

Static Weighbridges:

  • OIML Class III: Most common for trade
  • Typical accuracy: ±0.1% to ±0.2% of applied load
  • Minimum division: 10 to 50 kg

Example:

  • 60-tonne capacity weighbridge
  • Accuracy: ±0.1%
  • At 60 tonnes: ± 60 kg maximum error
  • Division: 20 kg (weight displayed in 20 kg increments)

Operation and Best Practices

Proper Weighing Procedure

  1. Vehicle approaches at slow speed
  2. Complete stop on platform (all wheels on deck)
  3. Engine off (reduces vibration)
  4. Driver remains in cab or exits (consistent procedure)
  5. Wait for stable reading (indicator shows stability)
  6. Record weight and vehicle details
  7. Exit platform completely before next vehicle

Avoiding Errors

Positioning:

  • Ensure entire vehicle on platform
  • Centered on platform
  • Not touching guardrails or barriers

Conditions:

  • Dry platform (water adds weight)
  • No wind loading (close doors/gates)
  • Stable ambient conditions

Maintenance:

  • Regular calibration
  • Keep platform clean and free of debris
  • Inspect for damage or binding
  • Check load cell connections

Maintenance Requirements

Daily/Weekly Checks

  • Visual inspection of platform
  • Check for debris accumulation
  • Verify indicator zero and operation
  • Clean platform surface
  • Check approach ramps for damage

Monthly Inspections

  • Load cell cable inspection
  • Junction box inspection
  • Check for rust or corrosion
  • Verify no binding or obstructions
  • Test with known weight

Annual Maintenance

  • Professional calibration: Using certified test weights
  • Comprehensive inspection: All components
  • Legal verification: If required for trade
  • Load cell testing: Resistance and output checks
  • Structural inspection: Welds, bolts, cracks
  • Electronics check: Indicator, cables, connections

Cost Considerations

Initial Investment

Pit-Type Installation:

  • Equipment: $30,000 - $100,000
  • Civil works: $20,000 - $80,000
  • Total: $50,000 - $180,000

Surface-Mounted Installation:

  • Equipment: $30,000 - $100,000
  • Civil works: $10,000 - $30,000
  • Total: $40,000 - $130,000

Factors Affecting Cost:

  • Capacity and size
  • Number of load cells
  • Platform material (steel vs. concrete)
  • Electronics and software features
  • Site preparation requirements
  • Local labor and material costs

Operating Costs

  • Calibration: $500 - $2,000 annually
  • Maintenance: $1,000 - $3,000 annually
  • Verification fees: $500 - $1,500 annually (if required)
  • Repairs: Variable, depending on usage
  • Electricity: Minimal ($100 - $300 annually)

Summary

Weighbridges are critical infrastructure for bulk material handling and transport operations. Key considerations include:

Installation Type Selection:

  • Pit-type: Space-efficient but higher cost and maintenance
  • Surface-mounted: Lower cost, easier maintenance, requires more space
  • Portable: Flexible but limited capacity

Operational Requirements:

  • Proper vehicle positioning
  • Regular calibration and maintenance
  • Environmental protection
  • Accurate record keeping

Investment Factors:

  • Initial cost vs. long-term requirements
  • Maintenance accessibility
  • Site conditions and constraints
  • Accuracy and capacity needs

Key Takeaways

  • Three main types: pit-mounted, surface-mounted, and portable
  • Pit-type offers space efficiency but requires extensive civil works and vigilant maintenance
  • Surface-mounted provides easier maintenance and lower installation cost but needs more space
  • Portable weighbridges offer flexibility for temporary applications
  • Capacity typically ranges from 30 to 150 tonnes
  • Static weighbridges achieve ±0.1-0.2% accuracy for commercial applications
  • Regular calibration and maintenance essential for accuracy and longevity
  • Proper installation and site selection critical for optimal performance
